# Introduction to accounting

Financial statements contains three components.



### Balance sheet 

(This is a **snapshot** of the company at the **end** of the finanical period)

- **Assets**
  - Current assets
  - Non-current assets
- **Liability**
  - Current liabilities
  - Non-current liabilities
- **Equity**



### Income statement

(This is a **record** of the company **over** the finanical period)

- **Revenue**

- **Expenses**

  - **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**
    
  Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS
    
  - **Operating Expenses**
    
    - Sales and marketing
    - General and administrative
    - Depreciation (straight line)
    
    EBIT = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses

  - **Interests and Taxes**

    Profit = EBIT - Interests and Taxes

- **Profit (or Net Income)**



### Cash flow statement

(This is a **record** of the company **over** the finanical period)

- Cash flow from operating activities
- Cash flow from financing activities
- Cash flow from investing activities
- Net cash flow
