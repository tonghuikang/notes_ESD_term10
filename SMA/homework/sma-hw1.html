The function $g(x)$ will be
$\frac{1}{2} e^\frac{x}{2}$ for $x\geq0$ and $0$ for $x<0$
with $c = 2$

We have calculated that

  • $c \cdot g(1) > f(1)$
  • $c \cdot g(x)$ does not intersect with $f(x)$ for $x>0$

Therefore $c \cdot g(x) \geq f(x) \enspace \forall x$

PDF $g(x) = \frac{1}{2} e^\frac{x}{2}$ for $x \geq 0$
CDF $G(x) = 1-e^{-x/2}$ for $x>0$
CDF_inv $G^{-1}(x) = -2\log(1-y)$

Question 5¶

part a¶

$$ \begin{align} \tilde{U} &= F_Y(a)(1-U) + F_Y(b)U \\ \tilde{U} &= F_Y(a) - F_Y(a)U + F_Y(b)U \\ \tilde{U} &= F_Y(a) + \left[F_Y(b) - F_Y(a)\right]U \\ \end{align} $$

We can see that $\tilde{U}$ is uniformlly distributed between $F_Y(a)$ and $F_Y(b)$.

part b¶

$$ \begin{align} X &= F_Y^{-1}(\tilde{U}) \\ F_Y(X) &= \tilde{U} \\ F_Y(X) &= F_Y(a) + \left[F_Y(b) - F_Y(a)\right]U \\ P(X \leq x) &= F_Y(a) + \left[F_Y(b) - F_Y(a)\right]U \\ P(X \leq x) &= P(Y \leq x | a \leq Y \leq b) \end{align} $$

$F_Y(X)$ is the cdf of $X$

part c¶

For exponential distribution:
PDF $f(x) = e^{-x}$
CDF $F(x) = 1-e^{-x}$
CDF_inv $F^{-1}(x) = -\log(1-x)$
